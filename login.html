Rural internet connections

GPS & IoT (Internet of Things) Devices: Farmers use GPS systems and IoT sensors to monitor soil conditions, weather, crop health, and irrigation needs in real-time. These sensors require a stable internet connection to send data to central systems for analysis and action.

Drones and Satellite Imaging: High-speed internet allows farmers to use drones or satellite images to monitor crop health, detect pests or diseases, and optimize irrigation and fertilization.
